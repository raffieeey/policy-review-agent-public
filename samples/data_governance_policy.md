# Data Governance Policy

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes the framework for managing organizational data assets throughout their lifecycle. It defines roles, responsibilities, and standards for data quality, security, and compliance.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party partners who access, process, or manage organizational data. It covers all data formats including structured, unstructured, and semi-structured data.

## 3. Data Classification
All organizational data must be classified into one of the following categories:
- **Public**: Data approved for external distribution
- **Internal**: Data for internal use only
- **Confidential**: Data requiring restricted access controls
- **Restricted**: Data with the highest sensitivity level

## 4. Data Quality Standards
Data must meet the following quality criteria:
- Accuracy: Data must be correct and free from errors
- Completeness: All required fields must be populated
- Timeliness: Data must be current and up-to-date
- Consistency: Data must be uniform across systems

## 5. Access Controls
Access to data must follow the principle of least privilege. Role-based access control (RBAC) must be implemented for all systems containing sensitive data.

## 6. Data Retention
Data must be retained according to legal and regulatory requirements. Retention periods must be documented and enforced through automated processes.

## 7. Incident Response
Data breaches must be reported within 24 hours to the Data Protection Officer. Incident response procedures must be tested annually.

## 8. Compliance
This policy must be reviewed annually and updated as needed to reflect changes in regulations and business requirements.
